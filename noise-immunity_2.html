Analog: Graceful Degradation

Every stage adds noise that cannot be removed. The original signal information is permanently mixed with noise. After many stages, the signal becomes unusable.

Digital: Regeneration

At each stage, we ask: "Is it above or below the threshold?" Then output a clean HIGH or LOW. Noise is rejected‚Äîthe signal is perfectly restored every time.

The Digital Advantage

With only two states (HIGH and LOW), digital systems have huge noise margins. As long as noise doesn't push a signal past the threshold, we can always recover the original data. This is why digital logic uses binary‚Äîit's maximally resistant to real-world interference.
